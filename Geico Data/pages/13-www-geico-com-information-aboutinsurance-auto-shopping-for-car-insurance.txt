How to Shop for Car Insurance

1. Gather the Information You Need to Shop for a Car Insurance Quote.

The first step in shopping for car insurance is to get a quote. To get an accurate car insurance quote, you'll need to provide some basic information. In addition to your name, address, and date of birth, some questions you may be asked include:

Type of vehicle(s) and how you use it

Vehicle Identification Number (VIN)

Driver's license number

Any other drivers in your household

Education and profession

Auto insurance history

Accident history

When purchasing auto insurance, you can choose how to receive your quote. You can get a quote online , call us at (800) 861-8380 to speak directly with an insurer, or work with a local agent to find the coverage that fits your needs.

Not sure about something? You can always save your car insurance quote at any step and come back later.

2. Determine How Much Coverage You Need

When shopping for the best car insurance, make sure you're comparing apples to apples. If you already have auto insurance, have your current policy details handy to understand your current limits and deductibles. You can also use our Coverage Calculator to help determine the amount of coverage that’s best for you.

Some things to consider when shopping for a policy:

Check the minimum coverage requirements for auto insurance in your state.

Consider carrying a higher deductible to lower your premium. A $500 deductible is a popular choice.

Collision coverage and comprehensive coverage provides protection for your car. If you lease or finance your car, your lender likely requires these coverages.

Liability coverage helps protect your personal assets in the event you are responsible for injuries or damage.

Uninsured motorist coverage protects you in the event of an accident involving an uninsured driver.

Rental reimbursement overage can help pay for a rental vehicle or other transportation while your vehicle is being repaired after a covered loss.

Emergency Roadside Service coverage can provide help with lockouts, jump starts, towing, and more.

Shopping for Car Insurance - FAQs

What is the average cost of car insurance?

The average cost of car insurance varies widely and depends upon your insurance company. There are many other factors that influence your rates, such as age, driving history, location, and vehicle type. Shopping around and comparing quotes from different insurers can help you find the best rate.

What is the cheapest car insurance?

The price of car insurance varies depending on individual circumstances, including driving record, location, and coverage needs. While some insurers offer lower premiums, it's crucial to consider factors beyond price, such as coverage limits, deductibles, customer service quality, and financial stability. When shopping for insurance, make sure that your policy fits your needs.

Do I buy a car first or insurance first?

You should buy car insurance first since most states and dealerships require proof of insurance before purchasing a vehicle. However, you will need to know the specific vehicle you are purchasing to provide its VIN during your quote. Securing insurance beforehand ensures immediate coverage, protecting against potential risks from the moment you drive the car off the lot.

Is there a grace period for buying car insurance when purchasing a car?

No, you must generally have insurance in place when you purchase a vehicle. However, many states and insurance companies offer a short grace period (typically 7 to 30 days) for buyers to add their vehicle onto their existing insurance policy. While requirements vary by location and insurer, it's best to secure insurance before obtaining the vehicle to ensure coverage.

Is there a way to get car insurance quotes without having a car first?

Yes, you can get a quote without owning a car by simply providing details about the vehicle you plan to buy. This helps you compare rates in advance and can help you select the coverages you want prior to purchasing your vehicle. If you don't plan on purchasing a vehicle but want to obtain coverage for yourself, you can also quote a non-owner policy.

Is $200 a month for car insurance bad?

Whether $200 a month for car insurance is high or reasonable depends on factors like your location, driving history, coverage level, and vehicle type. While it may be expensive for someone with a clean record and basic coverage in a rural area, it could be normal or even low for high-risk drivers or those needing additional coverages.
