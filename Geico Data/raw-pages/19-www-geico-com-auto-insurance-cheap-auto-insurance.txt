How to lower my car insurance rates 

The price you pay for car insurance depends on a number of factors. It can help to compare car insurance rates to find the most affordable premiums for you. However, there are many ways you could get cheaper car insurance —even with your existing policy: 

Add multiple cars to your policy : Adding multiple cars to your insurance policy can often lead to lower rates through multi-vehicle discounts. 

Bundling auto and home policies : We offer bundling discounts for loyal customers who purchase multiple policies through GEICO. 

Pay for your policy in full : GEICO may offer discounts to customers who pay their policies in full rather than in installments because it minimizes the risk of late and missed payments. 

Complete a defensive driving course : Insurers may offer discounts to policyholders who have successfully completed defensive driving courses because they demonstrate a commitment to safe driving practices and lead to a reduced risk of accidents. 

Keep a clean driving record : Drivers with no accidents or traffic violations typically receive lower premiums because their responsible, safe driving habits make them less risky to insure. 

Update your policy information regularly : Regularly updating your policy information, such as reporting changes in your driving habits or location, can help ensure you have coverage tailor-fit to your situation and potentially lower your car insurance rates. 

Review your limits and deductibles : By increasing your car insurance deductibles and reducing coverage to levels that align with your needs and circumstances, you can often enjoy lower premiums while still maintaining adequate protection for your specific circumstances. 

Inform us of safety and anti-theft devices on your car : GEICO rewards drivers with safety and anti-theft devices with discounts on their car insurance rates because they reduce the risk of theft or accidents and lead to better driving habits. 

Consider which type of vehicle you choose when buying a car : Cars with good safety ratings, lower repair costs, and less powerful engines tend to have lower insurance premiums, as they are associated with reduced accident risk and lower potential claim expenses for insurers.

What age does car insurance get cheaper? 

Car insurance generally becomes more affordable for drivers once they reach the age of 25. At this point, many insurance companies consider them less risky due to increased driving experience and more responsible behavior. Rates can continue to decrease throughout adulthood but will increase again when drivers reach the age of 70. It's important to keep in mind, however, that the specific age at which car insurance becomes cheaper varies depending on factors such as location, insurer, and individual driving history. 

Is minimum car insurance coverage enough? 

Minimum car insurance coverage may meet legal requirements, but it often falls short of adequately protecting you in serious accidents. It typically only covers basic liability for others' injuries and property damage. To protect your own vehicle and finances in more extensive scenarios, consider higher coverage limits or additional policies like collision and comprehensive. 

What factors can lower your car insurance rate? 

Lowering your car insurance rate is a priority for many drivers, and there are several factors that can help you achieve this goal. Factors that influence reduced car insurance premiums include maintaining a clean driving record, bundling policies, adopting safe driving habits, having low annual mileage, installing anti-theft devices or safety features in your car, having a good credit score, and choosing the right type of vehicle. Additionally, shopping around and comparing quotes from different insurers can help you secure the most affordable coverage for your specific situation.
