Determining Auto Insurance Rates 

It can be a hassle shopping for car insurance. It's hard to know who to trust and be confident you have the coverage you need, let alone at a good price. After all, you want a fair rate that is catered to you, right? GEICO likes to be transparent. You should know how we determine auto insurance rates. 

How are Car Insurance Rates Determined? 

Car insurance rates rely on the law of large numbers—a large collective group of policyholders shares the losses of significantly fewer policyholders. Within this large pool of people, insurers like GEICO are in a position to spot patterns and establish the probability of losses, such as accidents or claims. These patterns have an effect on your monthly premium. 

For more information, read our auto insurance fundamentals . 

How Does GEICO Determine an Accurate Car Insurance Rate for You? 

GEICO auto insurance rates are analyzed by GEICO actuaries, who assess potential risks from statistical data. A multitude of characteristics have been proven to accurately predict the odds that someone will have an accident. Insurance rates are then determined based on an individual's combination of high and low risk factors. You pay according to your risk of loss: higher risk = higher premium. 

What Factors Determine Auto Insurance Rates? 

Actuaries aim to determine the most accurate and fair car insurance premiums. Some factors that determine auto insurance rates include: 

Personal Factors 

When calculating your car insurance premium, insurance companies look at your personal profile to help predict how likely you are to file a claim. Some of these factors are beyond your control, but others can be improved over time, potentially leading to savings on your policy. 

A ge 

Younger drivers, especially teens, tend to pay more for car insurance because they have less experience behind the wheel. 

Driving Record 

Accidents, speeding tickets, and DUI convictions typically lead to higher premiums. But good news: If you keep a clean driving record, you may qualify for safe driver discounts and lower rates over time. 

Credit-Based Insurance Score 

In states where it’s allowed, insurers may use your credit-based insurance score as part of the risk assessment. Drivers with strong credit often pay less, so maintaining healthy credit habits could help reduce your premium. 

Your Car 

What you drive matters just as much as how you drive. Your vehicle’s make, model, safety features, and even repair costs can all influence what you’ll pay for coverage because they can impact the cost of a claim. 

Vehicle Type, Age, and Value 

Insurers consider the cost of repairing or replacing your car. That means newer or high-end vehicles typically come with higher premiums, while older or less expensive models may be more affordable to insure. 

Safety Features 

If your car comes with certain safety features, you may qualify for discounts. These features help reduce the chances of a claim, which benefits both you and your insurer. 

Cost of Repairs and Parts 

Vehicles with specialized parts or high repair costs can lead to more expensive claims. That’s why cars with rare or imported components may cost more to insure. 

Location and Usage 

Where you live and how often you drive both play a big part in determining your rate. From Zip code to mileage, insurers take your everyday driving habits into account. 

Location and Zip Code 

Living in a densely populated area with more traffic, accidents, or vehicle thefts can increase your premium. Weather matters too—regions prone to hail, flooding, or hurricanes may carry added risk. 

Annual Mileage and Vehicle Use 

The more time you spend on the road, the greater the chance of a claim. Drivers who use their car for long daily commutes or business purposes may pay more than those who drive occasionally. If you log fewer miles than average, you may be eligible for a low-mileage discount. 

Coverage and Policy Choices 

You have more control over your rate than you might think. The types of coverage you choose, your deductibles, and coverage limits all impact what you’ll pay. 

Coverage Types and Limits 

Higher coverage limits and optional protections like collision and comprehensive coverage offer more protection, but they also increase your premium. Think about how much coverage you really need before purchasing a policy. 

Deductibles 

Raising your deductible can lower your monthly premium, but you’ll pay more out of pocket if you need to file a claim. It’s all about finding the right balance for your budget. 

Optional Add-ons 

Optional car insurance coverages, such as Emergency Roadside Service or Rental Car Reimbursement, can add convenience and peace of mind. Just keep in mind that they’ll add to the total cost of your policy, but they can also save you money overall by helping you avoid unexpected out-of-pocket expenses when you need them most. 

Discounts and Ways to Save on Car Insurance Premiums 

While some factors that affect your rate can’t be changed, there are still plenty of ways to save on your car insurance. GEICO offers a variety of discounts that could help lower your rate. 

Common Insurance Discounts 

Looking to trim your premium? You might qualify for one or more of these car insurance discounts : 

Multi-Policy Discount : Bundle your auto insurance with a renters, condo, or homeowners policy to save more. 

Good Student Discount : If you’re a full-time student with good grades, you could see lower rates. 

Multi-Vehicle Discount : Insure more than one car with GEICO and get a discount on each. 

New Vehicle Discount : Driving a newer car? You may qualify for extra savings. 

Paid in Full Discount : Pay your policy in full upfront and skip the monthly installment fees. 

Usage-Based Insurance Programs 

Safe driving pays off—literally. With GEICO’s DriveEasy program, your driving habits are monitored through the GEICO Mobile app to help determine how safely you drive. If you practice good driving behavior, like smooth braking and staying off your phone, you may earn significant savings on your premium. It’s a smart way to take control of what you pay. 

Don't Worry—Your car Insurance Rate isn't Static. 

Not only is being a safe driver important for obvious reasons, but it enables you to have a lower premium. Know that through the many changes of your life, your rates may change. GEICO wants to reward you with affordable car insurance for your improved driving record, your car's safety features, and other safe behaviors on the road. Get a free car insurance quote or evaluate your policy regularly to see how much you could save. 

FAQ About How Insurance Rates are Determined 

What factors affect car insurance rates? 

Several factors go into determining your car insurance premium, including your driving history, where you live, the type and age of your vehicle, the coverage you select, and how many miles you drive each year. Insurance companies use this information to help assess risk and estimate how likely you are to file a claim. 

Does credit affect car insurance rates? 

In many states, insurers use something called a credit-based insurance score to help predict the likelihood of future claims. It’s not the same as your FICO score, and it doesn’t involve checking your full credit report. Some states don’t allow this practice, so whether credit affects your car insurance rate depends on where you live. 

Does age affect car insurance rates? 

It can! Younger drivers—especially teens—usually pay more because they have less experience on the road. As you get older, your rates may go down if you maintain a clean driving record. However, premiums can increase again later in life. 

Does the type of car you drive affect insurance rates? 

The make, model, year, typical repair costs, and safety features of your vehicle all influence your insurance rates. High-performance or luxury cars usually come with higher premiums because they cost more to repair or replace, while older or less expensive models usually have more affordable premiums. 

Does the color of your car affect your insurance rate? 

No, the color of your car does not affect your auto insurance rate. It doesn’t matter if your car is red, blue, black, or bright green—insurers don’t use color when calculating premiums. What really matters are things like the car’s make, model, safety features, and repair costs. So go ahead and pick your favorite color—it won’t change what you pay. 

How much does a traffic ticket affect car insurance rates? 

It depends on the violation. A minor speeding ticket might cause a small increase, but serious infractions—like reckless driving or running a red light—can lead to bigger changes. Please note that any ticket may disqualify you from receiving safe driver discounts. 

How long will most driving violations affect your car insurance rates? 

Most violations stay on your driving record for about 3 to 5 years, though it varies by state and insurance company. The good news? If you keep a clean record moving forward, your rates can start to go back down over time. 

Does a DUI affect car insurance rates? 

Yes, a DUI can have a major impact on your car insurance rates. It often results in significantly higher premiums due to increased risk, and may require you to file an SR-22 in certain states. The effects can last for years and may limit which car insurance options are available to you. 

Can I lower my car insurance rate without changing coverage? 

Yes! Keeping a clean driving record, asking about available discounts, and adjusting your deductible are all smart ways to save on car insurance without changing your coverage.
